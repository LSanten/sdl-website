Papers and Sources

This is a collection of papers related to the environmental impact of clothing

  • July 30, 2019

This is an assortment of papers that found useful related to the topic of clothing, laundering, and the general effects of laundry habits on the environemnt.
FREQUENTLY HIGH IMPACT IN USE PHASE refers to papers that emphasize the high impact of clothing during the use phase.
The heading IMPACT CHANGE THROUGH BEHAVIOR CHANGE refers to papers that show that a relatively small behavior laundry behavior change can lead to a great change in clothing's impact.

Frequently High Impact in Use Phase

Muthu, S. S. "Environmental impacts of the use phase of the clothing life cycle." Handbook of Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) of Textiles and Clothing. 2016. 93-102.

Allwood, Julian M., et al. "Well dressed." The present and future sustainability of clothing and textiles in the United Kingdom 1 (2006).

Madsen, Jacob, et al. "Mapping of Evidence on Sustainable Development Impacts that Occur in Life Cycles of Clothing: A Report to the Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs." Environmental Resources Management (ERM) Ltd. Defra, London (2007).

Cotton Incorporated “Life Cycle Assessment of Cotton, Fiber and Fabric - Full Report (2012).

Hackett, Tara. "A Comparative Life Cycle Assessment of Denim Jeans and a Cotton T-Shirt: The Production of Fast Fashion Essential Items From Cradle to Gate." (2015).

Strauss, Levi. "A Product Lifecycle Approach to Sustainability." San Francisco, USA (2009): 28.

Laitala, Kirsi, Ingun Klepp, and Beverley Henry. "Does Use Matter? Comparison of Environmental Impacts of Clothing Based on Fiber Type." Sustainability 10.7 (2018): 2524.
